Step 1: Open Terminal 1 (Type this FIRST)

sudo tcpdump -i lo -c10 -nn -vvv port 12345
Press Enter - It will say "listening on lo" and wait.

Step 2: Open Terminal 2 (Type this SECOND)

nc -lvk 12345

Press Enter - It will say "listening on [any] 12345"

Step 3: Open Terminal 3 (Type this THIRD)

telnet 127.0.0.1 12345

Press Enter - Connection will happen immediately.

Step 4: Watch Terminal 1
You will see the 3-way handshake packets appear.

text
Packet 1: Flags [S]   (SYN)
Packet 2: Flags [S.]  (SYN-ACK)
Packet 3: Flags [.]   (ACK)
Step 5: To exit

Terminal 3: Type Ctrl+] then quit then Enter

Terminal 2: Press Ctrl+C

Terminal 1: It will auto-stop after 10 packets or press Ctrl+C

For requirements a, b, c (run in Terminal 1):
Requirement	Command (Type in Terminal 1)
a) Filter any 10 packets	sudo tcpdump -i any -c10 -nn
b) Filter by IP	sudo tcpdump -i any -c5 -nn host 192.168.25.68
c) Verbose output	sudo tcpdump -i any -c10 -nn -vvv
