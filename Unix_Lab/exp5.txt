Process Management Commands

cmd :
fg -> Bring a stopped or background job to the foreground
Example: fg , fg %1 , fg jobname

& -> Run a job in the background (ampersand at end of command)
Example: sleep 100 & , gedit & , firefox &

Ctrl+Z -> Suspend the currently running foreground job (keyboard shortcut)
Example: Press Ctrl+Z while a command is running

bg -> Resume a suspended job and run it in the background
Example: bg , bg %1

top -> Display all running processes with real-time updates
Example: top , top -u username , Press 'q' to exit

ps -> Display process status (list currently running processes)
Example: ps , ps ux , ps aux , ps -ef

pstree -> Display running processes as a tree (shows process hierarchy)
Example: pstree , pstree -p , pstree username

nice -> Launch a process with modified scheduling priority (lower priority)
*Example: nice -n 10 ./script.sh , nice -10 command , nice -n 19 command*

renice -> Change scheduling priority of an already running process
*Example: sudo renice -n 5 -p 1234 , renice +10 -u username*

kill -> Terminate a process by specifying its PID
*Example: kill 1234 , kill -9 1234 , kill -TERM 1234*

killall -> Terminate all processes by name
*Example: killall firefox , killall -9 chrome , killall sleep*
