Process Management Commands

cmd :
fg -> Brings a background or suspended job to the foreground
Example: fg , fg %1 , fg jobname

& -> Runs a command in the background (placed at end of command)
Example: sleep 100 & , gedit & , firefox &

Ctrl+Z -> Suspends the currently running foreground process
Example: Press Ctrl+Z while a command is running

bg -> Resumes a suspended job in the background
Example: bg , bg %1

top -> Displays real-time running processes and system usage
Example: top , top -u username , Press 'q' to exit

ps -> Displays snapshot of current running processes
Example: ps , ps ux , ps aux , ps -ef

pstree -> Displays processes in a tree format (parent-child hierarchy)
Example: pstree , pstree -p , pstree username

nice -> Starts a process with adjusted priority (niceness value)
Example: nice -n 10 ./script.sh , nice -10 command , nice -n 19 command

renice -> Changes priority of a running process
Example: sudo renice -n 5 -p 1234 , renice +10 -u username

kill -> Terminates a process using its PID
Example: kill 1234 , kill -9 1234 , kill -TERM 1234

killall -> Terminates all processes by name
Example: killall firefox , killall -9 chrome , killall sleep
